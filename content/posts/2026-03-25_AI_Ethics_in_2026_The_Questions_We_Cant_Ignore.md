# AI Ethics in 2026: The Questions We Can't Ignore

As developers building with AI, we can't outsource the ethics to someone else. These are the questions the industry is wrestling with and that you should understand.

## Bias and Fairness

AI systems trained on historical data inherit historical biases. This matters when AI makes consequential decisions:

- **Hiring algorithms** that disadvantage certain demographics
- **Credit scoring** that perpetuates historical discrimination  
- **Medical AI** trained on non-diverse datasets
- **Criminal justice** tools with documented racial bias

The technical work: bias auditing, diverse training data, regular disparate impact testing. The organizational work: who's responsible when the model is biased?

## Deepfakes and Information Integrity

AI-generated video, audio, and images are now indistinguishable from real content. The implications:

- Political misinformation at unprecedented scale
- Non-consensual intimate imagery
- Evidence manipulation in legal proceedings
- Undermining trust in all media

Technical mitigations (watermarking, content authentication) are nascent. Legal frameworks are lagging. The problem is accelerating faster than solutions.

## Labor Displacement

AI is automating tasks across knowledge work:
- Code generation (affects developers)
- Content writing (affects writers)
- Data analysis (affects analysts)
- Customer service (affects support staff)

The optimistic view: new jobs emerge, productivity increases raise living standards.
The realistic view: transition periods cause real human suffering. Affected workers need support.

As developers building these tools: we're not neutral. We're active participants in this transition.

## AI Safety (The Long Game)

The technical AI safety community is working on:
- **Alignment:** Making AI systems actually do what we want
- **Interpretability:** Understanding why models output what they do
- **Robustness:** Making models that don't break under distribution shift
- **Corrigibility:** AI systems that allow themselves to be corrected

These are hard unsolved problems. Progress is happening but capability advances are outpacing safety advances in some areas.

## What Developers Should Do

1. **Ask "who is harmed?" for features you build**
2. **Advocate for impact assessments before deployment**
3. **Document your training data provenance**
4. **Build in human oversight for high-stakes decisions**
5. **Stay informed** — these aren't just academic questions

We're building the infrastructure of a transformed society. The ethics matter because the systems matter.
